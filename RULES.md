# Noble Dynasty - Game Rules

## Overview

**Noble Dynasty** is a strategic worker placement game where players compete to build influence, wealth, and power in a medieval city. Players command noble houses, manipulate the court, deceive rivals, and forge their dynasty over 12 rounds. The player with the most **Victory Points** at the end wins.

## Game Setup

### Players
- **3-4 players** supported
- Each player starts with:
  - **5 gold**
  - **1 political influence**
  - **2 servants**
  - **0 victory points**

### Action Slots
- **3 players**: 2 action slots per location
- **4 players**: 3 action slots per location

### Starting Phase
Before the first round, each player selects one starting advantage:

#### Starting Choices

1. **Noble Heritage**
   - Rewards: 3 gold, 2 political influence
   - Best for players who want early voting power

2. **Merchant Background**
   - Rewards: 8 gold
   - Best for players who want economic flexibility

3. **Military Service**
   - Rewards: 2 gold, 1 political influence, Guardhouse building
   - Provides assassination protection early

4. **Scholar's Path**
   - Rewards: 1 gold, Library building
   - Grants free news cards each round

5. **Artisan Guild**
   - Rewards: 6 gold, Manufactory building
   - Produces extra goods each round

6. **Court Connections**
   - Rewards: 4 gold, 3 political influence, 2 henchman cards
   - Strong political start with special actions

## Round Structure

Each of the **12 rounds** follows this sequence:

### Phase 1: Mayor Election

All players vote for who will be **Mayor** this round.

#### Voting Rules
- Players vote in turn order
- Each player's vote is worth their **political influence**
- Players can vote for themselves
- Players must vote for someone

#### Determining the Mayor
- Player with the most votes becomes Mayor
- **Ties**: Current Mayor wins if tied
- **Otherwise**: Player closest clockwise from current Mayor wins

#### Mayor Benefits
- Automatically receives the **Mayor role**
- Selects the **policy** for the round
- Takes the first action in placement phase

### Phase 2: Role Selection

Players select roles that grant special abilities for the round.

#### Selection Order
- Mayor is automatically assigned the **Mayor** role
- Remaining players select in **counter-clockwise order** from Mayor
- Each role can only be selected once per round

#### Available Roles

1. **Mayor** (automatic for election winner)
   - Political leader who enacts policies
   - Goes first in action placement

2. **Prospector**
   - Generates gold
   - **Cannot deposit VPs this round**

3. **Producer**
   - Your buildings produce **+1 goods** this round

4. **Architect**
   - Construction costs reduced
   - Can place servants on **occupied** build spaces

5. **Thieves Guildmaster**
   - Can block one action space
   - Assassination costs **-1 gold**

6. **Merchant**
   - Enables player-to-player trades
   - Bonus trading actions

7. **Recruiter**
   - Grants **one extra servant** this round only

### Phase 3: Policy Selection

The Mayor selects one policy that affects all players this round.

#### Available Policies

1. **Taxation**
   - All players pay 1 gold per servant to the treasury

2. **Corruption**
   - Players may pay 3 gold for +1 influence
   - OR lose 1 influence to gain +2 gold

3. **Conscription**
   - Players may spend influence to recruit servants
   - Recruitment Office action is unavailable

4. **Subsidy**
   - Building construction costs reduced by 3 gold

5. **Martial Law**
   - All players lose 1 political influence
   - **Thieves Guild is closed**

6. **Censorship**
   - All players discard one news card
   - **Printing Press is closed**

7. **Embargo**
   - **Marketplace is closed**
   - Mayor chooses one goods type for all to discard

8. **Hands-off**
   - All players draw and play one henchman card immediately

### Phase 4: Action Placement

Starting with the Mayor and continuing clockwise, players take turns placing servants.

#### Placement Rules
- Players place **one servant at a time**
- Action slots are **limited** (2-3 per location depending on player count)
- Once all slots at a location are filled, no more servants can be placed there
- Players continue placing until all servants are placed
- After all servants are placed, the round ends

#### City Locations

##### 1. City Hall
- **Action**: Lobby to adjust political influence
- **Effect**: ±1 political influence per servant
- **Cost**: 1 gold

##### 2. Thieves Guild
- **Action**: Attempt assassination
- **Cost**: 5 gold base (affected by Thieves Guildmaster role and Guardhouse)
- **Effect**: Eliminate an opponent's servant or influence
- **Closed by**: Martial Law policy

##### 3. Marketplace
- **Action**: Trade goods for gold
- **Effect**: 1 good = 2 gold
- **Bonus**: Market Stalls building grants +1 gold per trade
- **Closed by**: Embargo policy

##### 4. Recruitment Office
- **Action**: Recruit servants (3 gold) or draw henchman cards
- **Effect**: Gain permanent servant or special action cards
- **Note**: Unavailable under Conscription policy

##### 5. Printing Press
- **Action**: Draw or play news article cards
- **Effect**: Manipulate game state with news
- **Closed by**: Censorship policy

##### 6. Production Hall
- **Action**: Produce goods based on buildings owned
- **Effect**: Generate trade goods from your buildings
- **Bonus**: Producer role grants +1 goods

##### 7. Construction Yard
- **Action**: Build buildings
- **Cost**: Varies by building (6-12 gold)
- **Discount**: Architect role reduces costs
- **Subsidy**: Subsidy policy reduces costs by 3 gold

## Buildings

Buildings provide permanent bonuses and can be constructed at the Construction Yard.

### Available Buildings

| Building | Cost | Effect |
|----------|------|--------|
| **Library** | 6 gold | Grants one free news article card per round |
| **Barracks** | 7 gold | Grants one free henchman card per round |
| **Guardhouse** | 8 gold | Increases assassination cost against you by +1 gold |
| **Town Square** | 8 gold | +1 political influence per Lobby action |
| **Market Stalls** | 9 gold | Increase gold gained from goods traded by +1 |
| **Trade Depot** | 10 gold | Trade one additional good; enables player trading |
| **Spy Network** | 11 gold | Place servant to peek at another player's banked VPs or cards |
| **Manufactory** | 12 gold | Produces +1 good each round |

## Goods Types

Goods are produced at the Production Hall and traded at the Marketplace.

| Good | Base Value | Description |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Grain** | 1 gold | Basic agricultural produce |
| **Cloth** | 2 gold | Woven textiles |
| **Tools** | 3 gold | Crafted implements |
| **Jewelry** | 4 gold | Precious ornaments |
| **Spices** | 5 gold | Exotic luxury goods |

## Resources

### Gold
- Primary currency for actions, construction, and recruitment
- Gained through Prospector role, trading goods, or special actions

### Political Influence
- Determines voting power in elections
- Gained at City Hall, through starting choices, or buildings
- Can be lost through policies like Martial Law

### Servants
- Workers placed on action spaces
- Start with 2, can recruit more
- Reset to full availability each round
- Recruiter role grants temporary extra servant

### Victory Points (VPs)
- Hidden resource that determines the winner
- Deposited throughout the game
- **Cannot be deposited** if you have the Prospector role
- Final scoring reveals the winner

## Special Cards

### Henchman Cards
- Special action cards gained from Barracks or Recruitment Office
- Provide unique abilities or bonuses
- Played for immediate effect
- Starting: 0 cards (unless Court Connections chosen)

### News Article Cards
- Manipulate game events or politics
- Gained from Library or Printing Press
- Can shift balance of power
- Starting: 0 cards

## End of Round

After all players have placed all servants:

1. **Servants return**: All servants return to their owners
2. **Locations reopen**: All closed locations reopen
3. **Roles reset**: All role assignments are cleared
4. **Policy expires**: Current policy is removed
5. **Round advances**: Move to next round

## Game End & Victory

The game ends after **Round 12**.

### Scoring
- Players reveal their accumulated **Victory Points**
- Player with the **most Victory Points wins**
- Tiebreaker: Most gold, then most influence, then most servants

### Strategy Tips
- Balance economic growth with political power
- Time your role selections strategically
- Consider how policies affect your strategy
- Don't neglect VP accumulation
- Watch your opponents' resources and buildings

## Summary of Turn Order

```
1. Mayor Election
   └─→ Vote (in player order)
   └─→ Tally votes
   └─→ Determine Mayor

2. Role Selection
   └─→ Mayor gets Mayor role automatically
   └─→ Other players select counter-clockwise
   └─→ Apply role effects

3. Policy Selection
   └─→ Mayor chooses one policy
   └─→ Apply policy effects

4. Action Placement
   └─→ Starting with Mayor, clockwise
   └─→ Place servants one at a time
   └─→ Continue until all servants placed
   └─→ End round when Mayor's turn comes again

5. Round End
   └─→ Reset servants, locations, roles, policy
   └─→ Advance to next round or end game
```
